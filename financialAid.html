Financial Aid

FAFSA

The Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) is a form completed by current and prospective college students to determine financial-aid eligibility.

FAFSA

The FAFSA often needs to be completed for federal and state grants, work-study programs, and student loans.

The application usually opens in October, but for 2023-2024 the application will open in December.

After you apply and once your information is processed, you will recieve a Student Aid Report (SAR)

On the SAR there is a figure called the EFC, which is the Expected Family Contribution and is a measure of your family's finanacial strength.

General Scholarships

Coca-Cola Scholars

The Coca-Cola Scholars Program Scholarship is a merit-based scholarship awarded to seniors in high school. It recognizes students leadership, service, and impact in their communities. 150 scholars are selected each year to recieve $20,000.

Learn More
Gates Scholarship

The Gates Scholarship is a selective scholarship that covers the cost of attendance for outstanding minority high school seniors from low-income households. The scholarship is awarded to exceptional student leaders and is intended to help them realize their maximum potential.

Learn More
Taco Bell Live Mas

Scholarship for students (16-26) passionate about creating a better future and making a positive impact in their community. Not an achievement-based, but rather a passion-based scholarship. Awards range from $5,000 to $25,000 and can be used for qualified educational expenses.

Learn More
Cameron Impact Scholarship

The Cameron Impact Scholarship is a merit-based, four year, full-tuition, and impact-driven undergraduate scholarship. It is awarded annually to exceptional high school students that have demonstrated interest in leadership, community service, extracurricular activities, and academics.
